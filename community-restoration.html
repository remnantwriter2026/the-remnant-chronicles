Community Restoration

Restoration as an Ethical and Generational Work

By Jimey Grant Northside Lafayette, Louisiana Community Chronicle

Politics cannot make a man "better." Community restoration does not start at the State Capitol — it starts in the mirror.

As we honor the legacy of Dr. Martin Luther King Jr. with Black History Month just around the corner, the air is thick with the usual political rhetoric. From the neighborhoods of the Northside of Lafayette, Louisiana, to the African American communities across the nation, the conversation often defaults to a lack of government funding, systemic barriers, and political advocacy. While I respect the monumental effort of Dr. King's career and the legal chains he helped break, we must confront a hard truth that politics refuses to acknowledge: politics cannot make a man "better."

We have spent over a century marching, protesting, and voicing opinions over microphones — yet the core of the struggle remains. From a societal perspective, the deepest problems in the African American community are not merely political. They are ethical and generational. According to the Bible, the fundamental issue is not a lack of civil rights; it is the presence of sin and the rejection of personal accountability.

Infrastructure vs. The Intellect

There is a prevailing myth that "better buildings" create better people. We are told that if we improve the roads, the infrastructure, and the physical surroundings of our communities, the behavior of the people will follow.

The Myth
Better Buildings, Better People

Improve the roads, the infrastructure, and the physical surroundings — and behavior will follow. Municipal funding can fix a broken community.

The Truth
Order Is an Internal Fruit

Proverbs 4:23 — "Keep thy heart with all diligence; for out of it are the issues of life." Only a transfigured mind (Romans 12:2) can change a man. No facility can outpace the chaos of its occupants.

You can build a million-dollar facility — but if the people entering it are still bound by a victimhood mindset, the facility will eventually reflect the chaos of its occupants. Order is not an external gift from the government; it is an internal fruit of the Holy Spirit.

The Law of Individual Accountability

We must be bold enough to speak the truth regarding the system. While it is true that there are underlying systems that have placed drugs, alcohol, and violence in our communities, we must look at what the Word of God says pertaining to this matter.

Galatians 6:7–8

"Do not be deceived — God cannot be mocked. Whatsoever a man soweth, that shall he also reap."

In 2026, no governmental agency, gang, or network puts a gun to a man's head and forces him to get high or live the lifestyle pushed by modern media. We must stop putting the blame on the system and the government and start looking in the mirror — judging the everyday decisions that we are making. We cannot hide our choices behind the brokenness of our communities, for the Word of God reveals that every secret intent is uncovered and exposed in His presence.

True accountability begins when we realize that despite the pressures of the system, the final verdict rests on our individual response — as we each prepare to give a personal account of our journey to the Creator.

The Ethical Turning Point
A Personal Challenge

Tonight, after you read this message — look at your neighbor and say one word: Decisions.

What decisions are you making that have kept you in the situation you are in? The best investment you can ever make is not in a political candidate, but in personal development and spiritual alignment. This is the renewing of the mind that the Apostle Paul addressed in Romans 12:2. If we do not detach from the systemic narratives that tell us we are perpetually oppressed, we will never possess the mental territory required to build.

Deuteronomy 30:19

"I set before you life and death, blessing and cursing — therefore choose life."

When a man decides to work on himself, to pursue legal living, and to operate in the gifts and callings God has placed on his life — the system loses its power over him. He begins to operate in the Kingdom authority that God bestowed upon Adam.

The Path Forward
01
Break the Cycle

Men of our communities must decide to break the generational curses of fatherlessness, poverty, and addiction.

02
Stop Marching, Start Building

We have marched enough and voiced our opinions enough. It is time to repent, regulate our own lives, and build.

03
Foster Unity

Foster and adopt a spirit of unity through the power of the Holy Spirit — not political alliance.

04
Change Yourself First

The only way to change our communities is to change ourselves first — through a relationship with Jesus Christ and personal development.

A Final Charge to Communities Nationwide

True "Black History" is not just a record of what was done to us — it is a testament to what God can do through us when we take accountability for our souls.

Community restoration does not start at the State Capitol. It starts when the men of our communities decide to break the generational curses of fatherlessness, poverty, and addiction — and begin to foster a spirit of unity through the power of the Holy Spirit.

The only way to change our communities is to change ourselves first. And the only way we can do that is through a relationship with Jesus Christ, positive decision-making, and personal development.

We have marched enough. It is time to build.

JG
